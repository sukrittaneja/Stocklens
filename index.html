GEM Analysis

Growth Equivalence Model · Reverse-DCF

💡 How GEM Works
›

Imagine there's only one other investor in the market. He's not very smart — he values every stock by running a DCF at a fixed 8% discount rate for exactly 10 years, assuming EPS grows at a fixed rate forever.

This means every P/E ratio he's willing to pay implies a specific growth rate he's betting on. GEM reverse-engineers that rate from the current PE.

The logic:

📌 Enter current PE → app looks up Implied Growth (what market expects)
📌 Enter EPS history → app calculates your Actual Growth estimate
📌 Compare the two:
  ✅ Actual > Implied → Undervalued — market underestimates the business
  ❌ Actual < Implied → Overvalued — you'd be paying for growth that won't come
  〰 Roughly equal → Fairly Valued

Step by step

1

Go to Screener.in → search your stock → Profit & Loss → EPS row. Note the EPS from 5 years ago and the most recent year.

2

Enter both EPS values. The app calculates CAGR automatically. Apply a 20-30% haircut (built in) to be conservative.

3

If CAGR looks wrong (COVID years, one-off gains), enter your own estimate in the Override field instead.

4

Enter the current P/E ratio (consolidated, fully diluted — from Screener.in). The app auto-looks up the implied growth rate.

5

Read the verdict. A positive gap means the market is underpricing the growth you expect.

⚠ When NOT to use GEM

❌ EPS is negative or near-zero
❌ Banks / NBFCs — use Price-to-Book instead
❌ Highly cyclical businesses (metals, commodities)
✅ Best for: growth cos, industrials, manufacturing, chemicals, capital goods, railways

Haircut explained: Historical CAGR assumes the past repeats. A 25% haircut means if past CAGR was 20%, you use 15% as your estimate — building in a margin of safety for uncertainty.

Business Quality

Promoter holding > 50%, pledge < 20%?

Financial Health All on Screener.in — Ratios tab or 10-year data table

ROE — healthy and consistent?
No fixed threshold — judge for the sector. Ideally stable or improving over 3-5 years, not just one good year.
ROCE — healthy and consistent?
Return on Capital Employed. More relevant than ROE for capital-intensive businesses. Look for consistency.
Net profit growing over last 3 years?
Screener.in → P&L → Net Profit row. Should show a clear upward trend, not just one good year.
Revenue growth > 12% over 3 years?
Screener.in → P&L → Sales/Revenue row. Profit growth backed by revenue growth is more reliable than cost-cutting alone.
D/E ratio — acceptable for the sector?
Manufacturing: ideally < 0.5. Infra/Capital goods: up to 1-1.5 may be fine. Banks: ignore D/E, use other metrics.

Valuation

GEM verdict — undervalued or fair?
Run GEM first in the Analyse tab. If overvalued by GEM, mark No. If you haven't run GEM yet, mark N/A.
PE below or near its own 5yr average?
Screener.in → Stock page → scroll to PE chart. Current PE vs historical average. Buying below historical average = margin of safety.

Momentum & Context

EPS growing last 3 quarters?

Screener.in → Shareholding tab → check FII and DII trend over last 3-4 quarters. Smart money moving in is a positive signal.
